Project 1 – Text Data: Social Media and Mental Health

Motivation & Research Question

Social media platforms contain large amounts of text that reflect users’ feelings and stress. Understanding textual patterns related to mental health can help identify risk factors at a population level.

Research Question: What textual features in social media posts are associated with negative emotional or mental-health-related content?

Dataset & Preprocessing

  • Collected text posts from a public dataset (e.g., social media or mental-health-related text).
  • Cleaned text by lowercasing, removing punctuation, and deleting stopwords.
  • Applied tokenization and calculated basic statistics such as word frequency and sentence length.

Methods

  • Computed sentiment scores for each post and extracted keywords related to stress, anxiety, or depression.
  • Grouped posts into negative vs. non-negative categories using sentiment scores or keyword rules.
  • Visualized distributions of sentiment and frequent keywords using bar charts and simple plots.

Key Results

  • Negative posts contained higher frequencies of mental-health-related keywords than non-negative posts.
  • Certain words (e.g., “tired”, “alone”, “anxious”) appeared much more often in negative posts.
  • Text-based indicators can highlight emotional states but do not directly represent clinical diagnoses.

Discussion & Limitations

  • Keyword- and sentiment-based methods are simple and can miss sarcasm, slang, and cultural context.
  • The dataset and labels may contain noise, and results cannot be generalized to all users or platforms.

Future Work & Impact

  • Combine basic text features with more advanced NLP models to capture subtle emotional cues.
  • Explore temporal patterns (posting time, frequency) to better understand changes in emotional states.
  • Insights may support large-scale mental health awareness but should not be used as diagnostic tools.
